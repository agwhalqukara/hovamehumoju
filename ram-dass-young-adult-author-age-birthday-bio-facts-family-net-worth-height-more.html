Dass personally and professionally linked with Timothy Leary at Harvard University in the early 1960s. Then known as Richard Alpert, he conducted research with Leary on the therapeutic effects of hallucinogenic drugs. Additionally, Alpert assisted Harvard Divinity graduate student Walter Pahnke in the 1962 "Good Friday Experiment" with theology students, the first controlled double-blind study of drugs and experiments. mystery.
