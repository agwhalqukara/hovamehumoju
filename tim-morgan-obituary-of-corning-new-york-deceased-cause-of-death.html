About Corning

Discover the Charm and History of Corning

Located in Steuben County, New York, Corning is a city nestled along the picturesque Chemung River. With a population of 10,551 as of the 2020 census, Corning is a vibrant community that offers a blend of natural beauty, rich history, and modern amenities.

Named after Erastus Corning, an influential Albany financier and railroad executive, the city has a deep connection to its past. Corning’s development can be attributed to the company that Erastus Corning invested in, which played a pivotal role in shaping the community.

Corning is not only known for its historical significance but also for its thriving arts and culture scene. The city is home to the renowned Corning Museum of Glass, where visitors can explore the fascinating world of glassmaking through interactive exhibits and demonstrations. The museum’s collection spans thousands of years of glass artistry, showcasing the beauty and versatility of this captivating medium.

For nature enthusiasts, Corning offers ample opportunities to explore the great outdoors. The Chemung River provides a picturesque backdrop for activities such as kayaking, fishing, and scenic walks along its banks. The surrounding countryside is dotted with parks and nature preserves, offering hiking trails and breathtaking vistas.

Corning is also a hub for innovation and technology, with several major corporations and research institutions calling the city home. This has led to a thriving job market and educational opportunities, making Corning an attractive destination for those seeking career growth and advancement.

Whether you’re a history buff, an art enthusiast, or an outdoor adventurer, Corning has something to offer everyone. Immerse yourself in the city’s rich heritage, explore its cultural treasures, and embrace the natural beauty that surrounds it. Corning is a place where past and present converge, creating a unique and vibrant community that is waiting to be discovered.
